## Here two strategies rivalize. The concept of Luke 2:23 is already mentioned in Exodus 13:2,
## but some literal details are introduced just in Exodus 13:12, namely "arsen" ("boy")---even if it is
## impicitly mentioned in 13:2 in "πρωτότοκον" ("first born") ... "εν τοις υιοις" ("among the sons"),
## actually more precisely as in 13:12. Luke, however, mentions the word "arsen".
## The same happens with the expression "τω κυριω" ("to the Lord"). Implicitly it is there in 13:1:
## "αγίασόν μοι" ("sanctify to me"), but it is mentioned by Luke with the same word as in 13:12.

## The first strategy is to find the first passage that is mentioned. This would designate
## Exodus 2:13 as the origin of quotation. The second strategy is to use the better literal match.
## Here it seems logical to use the second strategy.
